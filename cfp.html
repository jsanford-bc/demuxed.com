What

What is this?

After hosting a meetup for over a year and a half, we’ve become what is universally agreed on as the greatest ensemble of video engineers the world has ever known. That may be a tiny bit of hyperbole: but in all seriousness, we really have built a great group of engineers and we think it’s time for more people to join.

In an effort to grow our humble video engineering community, we’ve decided to put on a conference. There are events out there for content producers and distributors, but none of them have a singular focus on the underlying technology and what goes into building it. We’re looking for speakers with experience from across the internet video spectrum, from building a player to writing specs, to encoding, and everything in between those things. Basically, if you’re an engineer with experience working on video, you belong. We’re looking for talks that fit anywhere in the engineer-building-internet-video spectrum, but some potentially interesting topics include:

  • New formats
  • Encoding / Decoding
  • Player technology
  • Delivery
  • Color space theory
  • Tooling
  • Accessibility
  • Whatever else you think might be cool. Srsly.

We're accepting 3 "kinds" of talks: Lightning (3 minutes), 25 minutes, or 45 minutes. Lightning talks are going to be one after the other and with pretty strict time restraints, so these need to be rapid fire! Keep in mind that the 25 and 45 minute time slots include time for questions and answers, so be sure to take that into account when planning.

Who

Who's behind this?

This all started at the SF Video Technology meetup (we're clearly geniuses when it comes to naming) in 2013. Over time, the group grew to have engineers from some of the top video companies in the Bay Area, including Pied Piper. A number of those engineers started thinking about doing something bigger, and that's how VidTechCon was born!

Help

We’re happy to help! If you work in video but you’re not sure what to talk about, please reach out. We can help flesh out vague ideas or convince you that we desperately need you on stage talking about how you got a Babbage Analytical Engine to encode VP9. Basically if you have the tiniest desire to talk and you have any concerns at all, shoot us an email: sfvidtech@gmail.com. (just don’t use this to submit a proposal, use the form).
